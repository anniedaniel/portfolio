About

My name is Annie Daniel, and I love the Internet.

I am a student at The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill's School of Journalism and Mass Communication studying multimedia journalism. I also have a second major in political science.

As a journalist, I'm always looking for the best way to tell a story. As a data journalist, I love to dig out the story out of the numbers and bring it to life.

I am not an "idea person". As a web developer I love to build, create and execute pieces that are self-explanatory and serve a purpose.

I am an advocate for hacker journalists in every newsroom who are able to build beautiful web spaces while remaining conscious of the ethics and principles of journalism. I am also an advocate of government transparency and accessibility of data, resources and government activities.

Believe it or not, I'm not just a code monkey who builds cool things on the Internet. I'm mostly a suburban North Carolina girl who loves Krispy Kreme and "This American Life".

Check out my stuff! Tell me what you think.

ONA music

I was a participant of the Online News Association Conference and Banquet Student Newsroom where I was tasked with creating content and providing coverage throughout the conference. I was able to work with professionals from the field to create this interactive package. I was assigned with Atlanta music, and included concerts that weekend, maps, restaurants close to the venues and a video player to sample tracks from the performing artists.


Capitol Hound

For Summer, 2013, I was hired along with a group of seven other students to work in UNC's Reese Felts Digital News Lab housed in UNC's School of Journalism and Mass Communication. We were tasked with creating two, financially self-sustaining startup news products. I was the lead developer in creating Capitol Hound- a searchable audio index for the North Carolina state legislature. Click here to read our business plan, marketing plan and view our prototype to learn more.
